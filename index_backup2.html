Hi! I'm Kamil Konowalik - a botanist and evolutionary biologist working at WUELS.

About me

I'm Kamil and I work at the University of Environmental and Life Sciences in Wrocław. Currently, I'm working on the project Phylogeography and hybridization within Carpathian ox-eye daisies (Leucanthemum, Compositae) where I'm studying Carpathian endemic Leucanthemum rotundifolium and associated taxa using molecular markers and cytogenetics. I have worked on phylogenetics, population genetics, cytogenetics, plant morphology, species distribution modeling (SDM), and GIS. I'm interested in the evolution of plants and especially in their speciation. Thus I'm studying hybridization and polyploidy which are the most prominent forces linked to those processes in plants.
In my research, I focus mainly on the Asteraceae family, the biggest family of land plants with about 25000 species with an enormous variability. I like combining geographical and biological data thus main activities are centered on biogeography. Moreover, since my childhood, I like experiments so whenever I have a possibility I try to perform some experimental works. Privately I like gardening and traveling.
